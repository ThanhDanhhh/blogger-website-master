I like the feeling of going to the convenience store at night.

At night, take a walk around the convenience store, buy some miscellaneous items, a bottle of water, a pack of cakes, then sit back and watch Saigon at night when the streets have gradually quieted down. Some people come to the convenience store to shop, some come to study, work, or simply find a place to rest, gradually becoming a part of Saigon. When it's cold, make a hot pot or hot clam rice, add your favorite drink from the countless types on the counter, it's just enough for a delicious and convenient combo. Saigon never sleeps, and the convenience store has \gradually become a part of the breath, the rhythm of life of this city, and the memories that will never be forgotten.
